## Timebinding with Fred and Dave Truesdale


{{< figure src="fred-pohl2-tangent.jpg" alt="Frederik Pohl" caption="Frederik Pohl, ca. 1977." >}}


*From the blog team:*


We see that sf critic [Dave Truesdale](https://web.archive.org/web/20160416144951/http://fancyclopedia.org/dave-truesdale), well-known gadfly, is once again stirring up  [controversy](https://web.archive.org/web/20160416144951/http://file770.com/?p=15979). But we have long memories, and recall when Truesdale was just another neofan, pubbing his fanzine and gushing about the pros he met.


Truesdale recently reprinted an interview with Fred from his early zine, [Tangent](https://web.archive.org/web/20160416144951/http://fancyclopedia.org/tangent). See it in [Tangent Online](https://web.archive.org/web/20160416144951/http://www.tangentonline.com/interviews-columnsmenu-166/2216-classic-frederik-pohl-interview).


In this paragraphless introduction, Truesdale recalls interviewing Fred at a Howard Johnson’s in Eau Clair, Wis., in 1977:

### One Comment

- Neil in Chicago says:
*Eau Clair, Wis., in 1977* made me wonder if the date was wrong.  

I remember a great party in George R.R. Martin’s room at Couleecon about three years later, partly because it was such a memorable con, and partly for the egoboo Fred gave me in that conversation.  

The subject got around to *Dhalgren*, which Fred had bought after every other living editor who spoke English had rejected it.  I said, if you want to complain that it lacks things you are entitled to expect from something labeled “novel”, you have a good case, but when I read it I realized there were parts of real life I had never seen on a page before.  Fred said, “Yeah!  Right!”
