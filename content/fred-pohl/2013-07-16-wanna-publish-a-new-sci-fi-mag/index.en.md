## Wanna Publish a New Sci-Fi Mag?


![super_science_stories-1](super_science_stories-1.jpg)


If you’re among that large and growing fraction of our blog readerrs who never miss anything in the blog and never forget anything you haven’t missed, you may recall an occasional musing from me about how much fun (and also how much labor) editing [Galaxy](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619230010/httphttp://archive.org/details/galaxymagazine) and [If](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619230010/http://www.isfdb.org/wiki/index.php/Magazine:If) was.  Pay was putrid, work was unending, but it was the best job I ever had,  and if someone made me a comparable offer today I’d have a really hard time turning it down.


Well, no one has, but something is stirring in that general area.  Lately I’ve been going over the problems involved in starting a new magazine.  It would be called [Super Science Stories](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619230010/http://www.isfdb.org/wiki/index.php/Magazine:Super_Science_Stories), which is the name I christened one of the two magazines I created for the giant pulp house of [**Popular Publications**](/fred-pohl/2011-05-26-rolling-back-the-years-popular-publications/) when they gave the kid me his first editorial job.


It would use all reprints, swiping the idea from [Famous Fantastic Mysteries](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619230010/http://www.philsp.com/mags/famous_fantastic_mysteries.html), the magazine [Mary Gnaedinger](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619230010/http://www.sf-encyclopedia.com/entry/gnaedinger_mary) piloted for the Munsey group in the ’40s.  Pulp paper.  4-color cover.  128 pages.  Price somewhere around $1.95.   Lettercol and, in every issue, a truculent [**J.W. Campbell**](/fred-pohl/2009-12-03-astounding-the-campbell-years-riding-high/)-like editorial.  Sound like fun?  It does to me — with, of course, some other distinguishing traits I don’t want to talk about right now.


*Retrome, Satanas!*


I know I shouldn’t give it a thought, but if an offer got real, how could I say no?

Dear Fred,
The economics of this seem doubtful, newsstand sales difficult, demand for reprints questionable.
My experience running a small mystery press (Perfect Crime Books) has tempted me once or twice to launch a two- or four-times a year mystery magazine to be sold as a large paperback on Amazon, B&N and so on as well as on Kindle and Nook. But it’s a daunting prospect. The positives are that you can set up a title for zero cost at CreateSpace and Amazon, but once that’s done the production cost and Amazon’s cut (which is less than a traditional distributor’s) forces a price of at least $12 on a 250-page book. If you pay even insulting prices for stories and art, it’s almost certain to lose money. And judging by our sales of $2.99 Kindle editions vs. paperbacks, genre readers are more and more ebook readers. (Who will be the next generation of pulp collectors?)
Fred Pohl’s name as editor would make a difference for an sf title, but what would fill the magazine? No Jack Vances, no C.C. McApps, no Jack Williamsons.
Still, I hope you get something running.
I think I mentioned once before that I recurrently dream about walking into a newsstand (of the sort that doesn’t seem to exist anymore) and finding a rack with recent issues of IF,for some reason with a Pederson cover, and thinking “Wow, this is still going on!”
What a nice thought.
Best wishes.
