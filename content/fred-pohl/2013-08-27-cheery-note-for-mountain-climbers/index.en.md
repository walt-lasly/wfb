## Cheery note for mountain climbers

[Time lapse photography of the melting of Jorge Montt Glacier, Chile](https://web.archive.org/web/20160416221555/http://vimeo.com/33237197)
  

Mountains are getting higher.  Way down at the tip of South America, Patagonia’s tall mountains are each getting about an inch and a half taller every year.


This isn’t from tectonic activity.  It’s because of the rate at which their [glaciers are melting](https://web.archive.org/web/20160416221555/http://www.independent.co.uk/environment/climate-change/glaciers-melting-at-fastest-rate-in-350-years-study-finds-2261414.html).  As the weight of all that ice flows away the mountains bounce back up in compensation.
