Yes, it was *Bullard of the Space Patrol*. I have two copies — glad to
hear they are collectible. Another piece he wrote, and my favorite,
was "Lilies of Life" in *Possible Worlds of Science Fiction* edited by
Groff Conklin. It takes place on Venus in which all the humans and
natives who have left the jungle are sick with something called swamp
jitters which causes severe convulsions. The protagonists, who are
scientists, and infected themselves, are desperately trying to find a
cure. If they don't find one soon, they never will be allowed to
return to earth because the longer a person lives on Venus, the more
diseases they catch. It turns out that there is a very elaborate
series of steps of a biological cycle that produce a substance that
cures the convulsions, and the scientists have to puzzle out how it
works. The cure has something to do with the natives' nasty,
uncivilized habits. This is one of those stories which is not outdated
in the least, except for the fact that Venus is probably not habitable,
but any very hot, jungle planet would do.
