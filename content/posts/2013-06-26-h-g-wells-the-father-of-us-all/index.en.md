## H.G. Wells: The Father of Us All


{{< figure src="H.G.-Wells.jpg" alt="H.G. Wells" caption="H.G. Wells" >}}


[Herbert George Wells](https://web.archive.org/web/20170620033552/http://www.biography.com/people/hg-wells-39224) was born in 1866, way in the middle of the 19th century, but he began living in the 20th, or in the even later Nth, before he was much more than 20 years old himself.


The way he got there was the way so many of us who came afterwards did, by writing stories about things that had never happened or at least haven’t happened yet — that is, what is generally called science fiction.


He didn’t just write it now and then, either, but made it his practice to seek new kinds of science-fiction stories to tell.  Such subjects as [time travel](https://web.archive.org/web/20170620033552/http://manybooks.net/titles/wellshgetext92timem11.html), [interplanetary war](https://web.archive.org/web/20170620033552/http://manybooks.net/titles/wellshgetext92warw12.html), [personal invisibility](https://web.archive.org/web/20170620033552/http://manybooks.net/titles/wellshgetext04nvsbl10.html), giving [dumb beasts language](https://web.archive.org/web/20170620033552/http://manybooks.net/titles/wellshgetext94dmoro11.html) and bringing [idyllic world peace](https://web.archive.org/web/20170620033552/http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1434404447/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1789&creative=390957&creativeASIN=1434404447&linkCode=as2&tag=twtfb-20) to the human race were all his own until he shared them with other, lesser  writers and thus enriched the whole field of literature.
