## Bright Sayings of Bright People, No. 8: Kolbert


{{< figure src="elizabethkolbert.jpg" alt="Elizabeth Kolbert" caption="Elizabeth Kolbert" >}}

**“Happiness is a good thing; it’s just not the only thing.”
—Elizabeth Kolbert**


### One Comment

- Alan Kellogg says:
Happiness is situational, happiness is transient. Happiness is not a permanent condition that need be obtained once, but a sometime thing that passes as the conditions that gave rise to it pass. Happiness is conditional, a mood, a feeling, not a permanent state. Happiness is a cat with a catnip mouse.
When we forget that happiness is something to be pursued, not something to be possessed, is when we forget that life can be a varied thing, filled with feelings and moods varied in their influence and effect. When we pursue happiness as the only thing worth pursuing is when we forget all the other things that are worth pursuing, and make happiness into a fetish that controls and limits our lives.
Happiness is a toddler who just learned how to run.
Happiness is not the only thing worth pursuing, worth having, worth sharing. Happiness is not for yourself alone, but is best shared as best as you can share it. Happiness is no excuse for ignoring the pains and fears of others, nor is it a cure for anything that ails you. But if it makes you happy to help those in deep woe, then by all means help.
Happiness is a warm puppy.
Just some stray thoughts on happiness.
