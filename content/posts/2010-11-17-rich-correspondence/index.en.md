# The Way the Future Blogs


Frederik Pohl


## Rich Correspondence

![email](email.png)

As those of you who have been paying attention know, a man named Mark Rich wrote a libelous book, in principal about my old-time collaborator, Cyril Kornbluth,  but in fact filled with denunciatory — rumors, suppositions, bad guesses and occasional outright falsehoods — material about me.  Why this Mark Rich person hated me, I can’t imagine.  But you can’t read more than a few pages of any part of his rotten book without concluding that he does.


Anyway, I have begun the unpleasant job of showing that very few of his libelous statements about me are are really accurate and many are preposterously wrong.


And the other night I sent him an e-mail:


{{< poem >}}
I don’t know if you have noticed but in my blog, The Way the Future Blogs, I have begun the job of correcting some of the vast number of mistakes, bad guesses and. I think, occasional outright lies in your book.  (There will be a lot more of this.)

No matter what I think of you, I believe in letting both sides speak.  If for any reason you want to question anything I say, I will publish in the blog any remarks you care to send me (up to, say, 300 words.)

Frederik Pohl
{{< /poem >}}


And the next morning, I got this totally unacceptable reply:


{{< poem >}}
Hi, Fred,

I’m actually pleased that you’ll be correcting any mistakes, and elaborating on
bits of history that were not to be found elsewhere.

Cheers,
Mark
{{< /poem >}}


I must say this little Mark Rich creep baffles me.


**Tell me, Rich: Is what you’re doing now your hope for a dignified retreat?  You can’t have a dignified retreat.  It’s too late.  That bridge is  crossed.**


**When you write a whole book of such poisonous libels, you only have two options:  One is to defend what you said, or at least try.  As you, of course, can’t do that, if you have any honor or decency at all, you only have one choice left.  That is, you can make a very public apology and retraction and start trying to make up for all the harm you have done.

Frederik Pohl**
