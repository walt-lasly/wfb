## Deborah Webster’s Solution


![cat](funny-pictures-science-cat-does-experiments-on-you-for-revenge.jpg)

  
[Lolcats and funny pictures](https://web.archive.org/web/20091204224043/http://icanhascheezburger.com/)

Back last winter, Deborah Webster, of Meadowbrook, NSW, Australia grew concerned about the way many scientists seemed to feel about the animals they studied — as “stimulus-response-driven robots incapable of thought or feeling” — so she wrote [New Scientist](https://web.archive.org/web/20091204224043/http://www.newscientist.com/article/mg20026861.000-trained-scientists.html) with a suggestion: “Every scientist working in the field should be given a cat.”


That would be for training purposes.  Then, when fully trained in such matters as opening and closing doors, choosing the right cat food and providing a comfortable lap to sit on, the scientist could return to his laboratory with an enhanced understanding of his subject.
