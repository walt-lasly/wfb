# The Way the Future Blogs


Frederik Pohl


## Keith Laumer


Who Bashed People With His Wit, Then His Cane


{{< figure src="Keith-Laumer.jpg" alt="Keith Laumer" caption="Keith Laumer" >}}


The first manuscript by [Keith Laumer](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619220410/http://www.keithlaumer.com/) that I remember seeing was about an interstellar diplomat named [Retief](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619220410/http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0671318578/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1789&creative=390957&creativeASIN=0671318578&linkCode=as2&tag=twtfb-20),  which caused me to stop reading manuscripts that day to write the author a letter, telling him I was buying the story and adding, “Please write me more stories, lots more stories, about this guy, because I love him.” And Keith did it, too, becoming, I’m pretty sure, one of the three reasons why [If](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619220410/http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000KC5634/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1789&creative=390957&creativeASIN=B000KC5634&linkCode=as2&tag=twtfb-20), the magazine I published them in, won the Best Prozine Hugo for three years in a row. (The other two reasons? [Fred Saberhagen’s](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619220410/http://www.amazon.com/mn/landing/B000AQ26EC/?_encoding=UTF8&camp=1789&creative=390957&linkCode=ur2&qid=1348462314&sr=1-2-ent&tag=twtfb-20) [Berserker](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619220410/http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0441054951/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1789&creative=390957&creativeASIN=0441054951&linkCode=as2&tag=twtfb-20) stories, and [**Robert Heinlein’s**](/posts/2010-05-03-working-with-robert-a-heinlein/) serials.)


I felt pretty proud of myself for recognizing their worth so quickly, but I later learned that he had intended them as a series all along, but planned for the series to run in [Cele Goldsmith’s](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619220410/http://socialistjazz.blogspot.com/2012/07/cele-goldsmithlalli-interviewed-by.html) Amazing (and I can’t imagine why Cele, bright as she was, let them go). The thing about Laumer is, first, that he was great at satirizing people he had had a bellyful of, particularly Americans in the diplomatic service. (Keith had served a tour in Burma, which gave him much grist for his mill.) Second, that he had a keen sense of comedy, and, third, that he wrote quickly and well.


Not all of Keith’s sf was part of a series. He wrote stand-alone stories when the spirit moved him, some of them really good. There was one in particular — I’ve forgotten its name — which had to do with a time traveler who, leaving his family behind, travels a century or so into the future, where he finds a dreary, post-catastrophe world where his only companion is a nearly out of it centenarian who, when the time traveler mentions his name, sobs, “Daddy.” Corny, maybe, but it took me unawares to the point of actually bringing a tear to my eyes — something which rarely happens.


I left the Galaxy group shortly before Keith suffered the massive stroke that pretty much ended his successful writing career, but I did see him from time to time. Sadly, the wreck of his brain made him the legendary even-tempered man: mad all the time. It was a terrible fate for one as talented as he and, though he lived for twenty-some years after the stroke, he never regained the art of writing a Laumer story, and almost never managed to carry on a conversation without breaking into rage.

I always wondered what happened to Keith Laumer. He was one of my favorite sf authors, back in the day. It seemed like he could do any genre, and do it well: Space opera, time travel, alternate universes, comic sf and fantasy. All that talent wiped out by a tiny blood clot.
He still left us one hell of a body of work, though. I’m not all that big a fan of Baen Books, but I do appreciate the fact that they’ve made a fairly extensive sampling of Laumer’s work available as [free ebooks](https://web.archive.org/web/20170619220410/http://tinyurl.com/cz2po9b), and I strongly urge anyone unfamiliar with his writing to check out their website.
