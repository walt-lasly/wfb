## What Flowers Know — But Politicians Can’t Figure Out


![tulips](tulips.jpg)


If you’ve been ordering seeds for your garden lately you may have noticed a change in the packaging.  Seed packets usually come with a map or printed instructions on the back of the packet telling you when to plant them, depending on where in America to live.  This year the instructions have been changed by the Department of Agriculture, to reflect the fact that gardeners (as Professor Richard Primack of Boston University said in a Chicago Sun-Times story) “are well aware of the fact that temperatures have gotten more mild throughout the year, particularly in the wintertime.”  For that reason many seeds can be planted earlier now.


It’s nice that gardeners can use this information to make their plantings prettier.  It would be even nicer if our head-in-the-sand political leaders used that scientifically based information to start preparing now for the more violent and more widely occurring hurricanes, tornados and droughts that will come along with that global warning.
