## Tong Guide to Chinatown New York


{{< figure src="chinatownnyc.jpg" alt="Chinatown, New York City." caption="Chinatown, New York City." >}}


Just before World War II, I lived in an apartment in the giant (at the time) development called Knickerbocker Village, on Monroe Street in the shadow of the Manhattan Bridge, close to the foot of Manhattan island.  KV was only a few blocks from New York’s expanding Chinatown and I, usually along with some uptown friends, would walk over to it on Sunday afternoons, or at dinnertime on some other days of the week, and, while I knew something of the bloody history of the neighborhood as rival tongs fought for dominance, I didn’t know where the various home bases were.


Well, it has taken a while — try almost seventy years — but I do have some information of that kind now, and I hasten to share it with any blog buddies who happen to find themselves there now.  If you’re on Mott Street, you’re in the territory of the On Leong Tong, with its associated youth gang, the violent Ghost Shadows.  On Pell Street, it’s the Hip Sing Tong and the Flying Dragons (whose boss, Johnny Eng, “had moved into the heroin trade when the Italian Mafia began getting out of it”).


It is not recommended that you ask people you chance to run into on the street for more specific kinds of information.  The tongs are designed to get violent only to each other, because violence toward tourists is bad for business — countless souvenir shops and even more restaurants, almost all pretty good, not to mention, for instance, narcotics — but they can make an exception for visitors who might be, oh, say, cops.
