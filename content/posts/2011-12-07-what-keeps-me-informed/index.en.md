## What Keeps Me Informed

![New Yorker](new-yorker-cover.jpg)

[**TAD**](/posts/2011-10-13-planet-earth-almost-good-news-maybe/) says he’s tired of getting up-to-date scientific and political news from this blog, and can I recommend other sources?


I can tell you what I read every week, which is five weekly publications:  [Science](https://web.archive.org/web/20170707040924/http://www.sciencemag.org/), [New Scientist](https://web.archive.org/web/20170707040924/http://www.newscientist.com/),  [The New Yorker](https://web.archive.org/web/20170707040924/http://www.newyorker.com/), the Sunday [New York Times](https://web.archive.org/web/20170707040924/http://www.nytimes.com/)  and [Newsweek](https://web.archive.org/web/20170707040924/http://www.thedailybeast.com/newsweek.html).


Science is mostly pretty technical.  New Scientist is an English magazine and fairly expensive in the U.S., but you can read much of it online, and maybe some nearby public or college library gets it and will let you read it.  (If not, get on the one most likely to listen to you and tell them they should.)  If I could subscribe to just one magazine, this would be it.


The New Yorker may sound like a lightweight choice but its articles in depth can’t be beat, and often they are on scientific subjects.  (They also have great cartoons and much else.)


I also read about a dozen other periodicals and miscellaneous other stuff, but far the most of what I know and keep replenishing is from these five.
