# Letter from Virginia Mill to RAH (KHJ radio discussion)

*Virginia Mill warns RAH that A. E. Van Vogt and Kris Neville will be discussing his 1961 Seattle speech on Joe Dolan's nightly KHJ radio program on September 18, 1962, and invites him to be present.*

{{< figure src="mail.png" >}}

{{< typewriter >}}

<p class="letter-address">2318 Edgewater Terr.<br>
Los Angeles 39<br></p>
<p class="letter-date">Sept 10, 1962</p>

Dear Robert Heinlein:

Be prepared to defend yourself if you so desire. You are about to be torn to shreds on the night of September 18 1962 by A. E. Van Vogt and Kris Neville in discussion with Joe Dolan over radio station KHJ-Am-Am, Los Angeles (10 p.m. L.A. time). We or they heard the tape of your Seattle speech (summer, '61), and while you are very listenable (as in photogenic— perhaps I should say 'audiogenic'), there are one or two little things contained therein which we think will bear (and, in fact, beg) further consideration.

You are most welcome to be present. Mr. Dolan would be as delighted as would Neville and Van Vogt. Dolan is a rather phenomenal brain — Phi Beta Kappa, a lawyer, who conducts penetrating, socially conscious, cerebral interviews, at totally ad lib, for an hour at a time nightly on KHJ, five nights a week, followed by a short interview on TV and preceded a couple of hours earlier by a few-minutes-long editorial.

My haste in transmitting this note, likewise the shortness of notice, if rude, are, as far as I can see, unavoidably so. More to follow.

Yours,

{{< figure src="signature_mill.png" alt="Virginia Mill" caption="Virginia Mill" >}}

{{< /typewriter >}}

**Notes**

**Virginia Mill** — Virginia Mill was a member of the Los Angeles Science Fantasy Society (LASFS) in the early 1960s. She was active in the club's administration and fandom scene.

**A. E. Van Vogt** — A. E. van Vogt (1912–2000), prolific Canadian-American SF writer and a founding member of the Los Angeles Science Fantasy Society (LASFS). He lived in Los Angeles from the 1944 and was a major figure in local SF circles.

**Kris Neville** — Kris Neville (1925–1980), SF writer best known for the story "Bettyann" (1951). He lived in Los Angeles and was active in LASFS through the early 1960s.

**KHJ-Am-Am** — KHJ was a 50,000-watt AM radio station at 930 kHz in Los Angeles; in 1962 it operated as a talk/news station before its later transformation into a Top 40 outlet ("Boss Radio") in 1965.

**Joe Dolan** — Host of a nightly hour-long interview program on KHJ. Mill describes him as a Phi Beta Kappa lawyer known for cerebral, ad-lib interviews.

**RAH's Seattle speech (summer, '61)** — Heinlein delivered an address in Seattle around the time of the 1961 World's Fair; a tape recording of it was apparently circulating in SF fan circles by late 1962.
