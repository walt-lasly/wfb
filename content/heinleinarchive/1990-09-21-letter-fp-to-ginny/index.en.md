# Letter from Frederik Pohl to Ginny Heinlein (Florida)

*Fred Pohl writes to Virginia (Ginny) Heinlein from his home "Gateway" in Palatine, Illinois, responding to her plans to move to Florida after Robert's death. He gently weighs in on the various regions of Florida, mentions that he and Betty scouted the Keys, and confesses his own preference for the Hilo side of Hawaii's Big Island — though Betty's allergy to flowering ginger rules it out.*

{{< typewriter >}}

<p class="letter-address">Frederik Pohl<br>"Gateway"<br>855 South Harvard Drive<br>Palatine IL 60067<br>(708) 991-6009</p>

<p class="letter-date">21 September 1990</p>

Dear Ginny:

Are you sure you want to move to Florida? The climate's nice enough, but the Atlantic coast gets more congested (with retirees and Colombian drug dealers) every year, the Gulf coast is better (but the water tastes funny) and, although it's not noted for earthquakes, it does have the odd hurricane.

Betty and I checked out the Keys a year or so ago and pretty much liked them, but we're still searching for that great golden place. My own preference would be Hawaii, preferably the Hilo side of the big island, which has everything I want in a place to live . . . but unfortunately it also has flowering ginger growing all over and Betty's allergic to the stuff.

Anyway, good luck in your hunting. . . .

Bests,

{{< figure src="signature_fred_pohl.png" alt="Fred" caption="Fred" >}}


{{< /typewriter >}}

**Notes**

**"Gateway"** — The name Pohl gave his Palatine, Illinois home — a nod to his 1977 novel *Gateway*, which won the Hugo, the Nebula, and the John W. Campbell Memorial Award and is generally considered his masterpiece.

**Betty Pohl** — Elizabeth Anne Hull (b. 1945), SF scholar, editor of several anthologies, and longtime chair of the SFWA forum. She married Fred Pohl in 1984; her allergy to flowering ginger ruled out the Hilo side of Hawai'i's Big Island.

**Florida — Atlantic vs. Gulf coast** — In 1990 the Atlantic coast (Palm Beach, Fort Lauderdale, Miami) was heavily developed and associated in the press with the cocaine trafficking epidemic of the 1980s. The Gulf coast (Naples, Sarasota) was considered quieter and less congested.

**Hilo side of the Big Island** — The windward (east) side of Hawai'i island: lush, rainy, far less touristic than the Kona resort coast — exactly the combination Fred Pohl found appealing.

**Ginny's move** — Ginny Heinlein ultimately did not relocate to Florida; she remained in Carmel, California, at the address (PO Box 222278) that appears on other letters in this archive.
