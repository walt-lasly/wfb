# Letter from Algis Budrys to RAH (Chicon invitation)

*AJ writes eight months after RAH's November letter, apologizing for the long silence. He thanks RAH for an unspecified clipping, expresses concern for Ginny's health, and reports that Regency has finally acquired a major distributor. He announces that his family settled in Evanston in January — just before the birth of his fourth son, David. He asks RAH not to let the matter of the Vilnius photographs become an obligation, and looks forward to seeing him at the 1962 Worldcon (Chicon III, Chicago).*

<p class="letter-date">July 26, 1962</p>

Dear Bob:

Thank you very much for the clipping--I hadn't seen it; if I had, I think the attendant cry of joy would have been audible in Colorado.

I'm very sorry to hear about Ginny. Please tell her I'm sure that whatever it is has less fortitude than she does.

I'm also acutely aware of my rudeness in not having replied to your letter of October. I plead overwork on a scale I'd never experienced, and the mental flurry involved in taking on a complicated job without being fully qualified for it. I finally took a vacation last week, though, and sometime around last Thursday stopped jumping at loud noises and shying from shadows. Regency, meanwhile, has had the good fortune of picking up a major distributer in place of the haywire outfit it formerly enjoyed, so that you might even start seeing some of our stuff right out on sale as if we were a commercial publisher.

The family finally settled in here during January, about a week before David was born. Like all young Budryses, he is a cheerful, alert youngster who looks nothing like his brothers but nevertheless manages to resemble his family. This one has a square head and big ears, and dark brown eyes, and a face made for a tough grin, like my father's. Eddie is fine--better than she has been in years, if that's possible--and we all live here in Evanston at 824 Seward Street. It's a very big house, with extra room for visitors. The unlisted phone number is 864-3668.

The matter of the pictures shouldn't be allowed to twinge even slightly, of course. It was very kind of you to offer them, but for Pete's sake don't let this grow into an albatross--_that_ would make me very uncomfortable.

I hope to see you at the Convention, and hope even more strongly that our wives will have an opportunity to meet then. (I have reason to deduce that you might leave space in your luggage for another piece of bulky statuary, by the way the nominations came in last month.) Meanwhile, I'm tuning up to actually write you a letter of reply. Neither this interim response nor that one are going to find me in the least perturbed if you don't find time to write--I only wish you were busy with something more pleasant.

All the best,

{{< figure src="signature_aj.png" alt="AJ" caption="AJ" >}}

{{< /typewriter >}}

**Notes**

**Chicon III** — The 20th World Science Fiction Convention, held August 31–September 3, 1962 in Chicago. RAH attended and won the Hugo Award for Best Novel for *Stranger in a Strange Land*.

**"Bulky piece of statuary"** — A winking allusion to the Hugo Award rocket trophy. By July the Chicon nominations were in; AJ's "I have reason to deduce" is a polite way of telling RAH he has been nominated. RAH did indeed win.

**David Budrys** — AJ and Eddie's fourth son, born January 1962 in Evanston — roughly a week after the family settled at 824 Seward Street, just as RAH had predicted at the close of the November 1961 correspondence.

**Ginny's health** — AJ's concern about Ginny's illness is the first hint in this correspondence of health troubles that would continue to occupy the letters over the following years.
