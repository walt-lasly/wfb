# Letter from Hugh Hefner to RAH (Playboy first look)

*Hefner thanks RAH for kind words about "the house and party" — almost certainly a reference to the Playboy Mansion gathering around Chicon III (Chicago, August 1962). He is delighted to learn RAH is a long-time Playboy fan, and reports that "Spec" has told him Playboy will get a first look at RAH's future work.*

{{< figure src="playboy_logo.png" >}}

{{< typewriter >}}

<p class="letter-date">September 17, 1962</p>

<p>Mr. Robert Heinlein<br>
1776 Mesa Avenue, Broadmoor<br>
Colorado Springs, Colorado</p>

<br>

Dear Bob:

Thank you for the very nice letter and the kind words about the house and party. I assure you that you did not overstay your welcome — if I hadn't been having a first-rate time myself, just relaxing there and talking about everything and nothing, I would have begged off. It was a very great pleasure meeting you and learning that you are a long-time PLAYBOY fan. Though I couldn't come up with a title of one of your stories, when you asked me, I really have been a fan for a good many years, so learning that you, in turn, have been a fan of PLAYBOY really does have a great deal of meaning to me.

I understand from Spec that we are going to be getting a first look at your future work and that certainly is fine, because I would very much like to have some of your best in PLAYBOY's pages.

I hope that circumstances are such that our paths cross soon again.

Sincerely,

{{< figure src="signature_hugh.png" alt="Hefner" caption="Hefner" >}}

Hugh M. Hefner  
Editor-Publisher

{{< /typewriter >}}

**Notes**

**"The house and party"** — The Playboy Mansion in Chicago (1340 N. State Parkway), Hefner's celebrated residence and entertainment venue. RAH and Ginny were in Chicago for Chicon III (August 31–September 3, 1962), where RAH won the Hugo for *Stranger in a Strange Land*. The party was almost certainly hosted during or just around the convention.

**"Spec"** — Identity uncertain; likely a Playboy editorial intermediary or RAH's agent Lurton Blassingame using a nickname. The arrangement described — a first-look deal on RAH's future fiction — was a significant publishing commitment.
