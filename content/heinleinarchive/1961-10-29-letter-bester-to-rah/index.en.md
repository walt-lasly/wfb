# Letter from Alfred Bester to RAH (Holiday, "F&SF's loss")

*Writing from Holiday magazine on an undated Sunday — most likely late October 1961 — Alfie Bester graciously accepts RAH's refusal to write for F&SF: "it's F&SF's loss." He chides that there's no arguing with a Schulz cartoon, wonders if Ginny is still collecting emeralds, and recounts how a Natural History Museum curator showed him a 650-carat emerald crystal — and how he instantly devised and dismissed a hundred cunning plans to steal it for her.*

{{< typewriter >}}

{{< figure src="holiday_logo.png" >}}

<p class="letter-date">Sunday...</p>

Dear Bob:

If you don't want to, you won't; and it's F&SF's loss. There is no answer to a Schultz cartoon, unless I send a few pages from the Pogo books, which would be even more devastating. You recall, perhaps, the bookworm who sneered at the spelling and grammar in Webster's dictionary?

How is Ginny, and is she still enhancing her emeralds? Please tell her that I thought of her with mixed emotions when I was doing a piece on the Natural History museum a few months ago. The Hard Rock curator showed me a 650 carat emerald crystal which had just been donated to the museum, and in a flash I devised and dismissed a hundred cunning plans to steal it for her.

I was confused because I could conceive of no legitimate way in which she could show her gratitude if I did manage to pull off the theft for her.

Ever my best...

{{< figure src="signature_alfie.png" alt="Alfie Bester" caption="Alfie Bester" >}}


{{< /typewriter >}}

**Notes**

**"Schultz cartoon"** — Bester's misspelling (preserved from the original) of Charles Schulz, creator of *Peanuts* (1950–2000). By 1961 the strip was a national phenomenon; whatever RAH had cited as his reason for declining, Bester clearly found it unanswerable.

**Pogo / Walt Kelly** — Kelly's satirical comic strip (1948–1975), famous for its wordplay, political allegory, and literate humor. The bookworm who sneered at Webster's grammar is a classic early-1950s Pogo strip.

**Ginny's emeralds** — Virginia Heinlein was well known among friends for her love of emeralds. RAH gave her emerald jewelry on various occasions, and the stone appears in several letters.

**Holiday magazine** — A high-quality travel-and-culture monthly published by Curtis Publications (same house as *The Saturday Evening Post*). Bester contributed heavily to it through the 1950s and 1960s, writing long features on cities and regions worldwide.

**American Museum of Natural History** — The Hall of Gems on the museum's fourth floor houses one of the world's great mineral collections; the Morgan Memorial Hall of Gems was renovated in 1976, but major mineral donations were common in the early 1960s.
