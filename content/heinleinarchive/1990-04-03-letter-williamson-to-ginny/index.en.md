# Letter from Jack Williamson to Ginny Heinlein (biography)

*Jack Williamson writes from Portales, New Mexico — glad Ginny has recovered, reporting a Caribbean cruise, 10,000 words into a new novel, and signing sheets for Easton Press's* Mazeway. *He praises* Grumbles from the Grave *and encourages Ginny to write RAH's biography.*

{{< typewriter >}}

<p class="letter-address">JACK WILLIAMSON<br>Box 761<br>Portales, NM 88130<br>(505) 356-5608</p>

<p class="letter-date">April 3, 1990</p>

<p>Mrs. Robert A. Heinlein</p>
<p>PO Box 222278</p>
<p>Carmel, CA 93922</p>
<br>

Dear Ginny:

It's good news that you're finally over the never-get-overs.

Too bad you missed the Canaries trip, but I trust that you can find another.

With my brother, his wife, and my daughter Adele, I have reservations for a Caribbean cruise next month. What appeals most to me is a stop in Yucatan. I've never seen the Mayan ruins.

And life goes on. I enjoy teaching the science fiction class, and I have some 10,000 words of a new novel in the computer.

About signing those 2500 sheets for the Easton Press MAZEWAY, I was happy enough to do it. They were paying me a dollar a sheet, which struck me as pretty easy money.

I've been glad to see the good reviews of GRUMBLES.

And if you do go ahead with the biography. The world is full of people interested in Robert's life, and who can tell it better? I imagine your chief difficulty will come from the instinct for privacy that you and he shared. The way to make a life interesting is to tell the unvarnished truth, even if it may sometimes be something you would prefer not to say.

Best wishes,

Jack Williamson

**Notes**

**Jack Williamson (1908–2006)** — One of the longest-lived and most prolific SF writers in history, with a career spanning from 1928 to his death at 98. In April 1990 he was 81. He taught science fiction at Eastern New Mexico University in Portales for decades and remained actively productive until the end of his life.

**"Never-get-overs"** — A folk expression for a prolonged illness or grief from which one never fully recovers; Williamson uses it gently to acknowledge that Ginny had been seriously unwell.

***Mazeway* (1990)** — Williamson's novel set on a terraformed Jovian moon, published by Del Rey. The Easton Press produced a signed leather-bound limited edition of 2,500 copies; at $1 per sheet, the signing session earned Williamson $2,500.

**Easton Press** — A Connecticut publisher specializing in luxury leather-bound collector editions of classic and contemporary SF, philosophy, and literature, often in signed limited runs.

***Grumbles from the Grave*** — Published November 1989; by April 1990 it had received considerable press attention. Williamson's praise of the reviews leads naturally into his encouragement that Ginny write a full biography.

**"Instinct for privacy"** — Williamson's tactful warning that a candid biography of RAH would require revealing things RAH kept deliberately private — a real obstacle that Ginny never fully overcame; no authorized biography was completed during her lifetime.
