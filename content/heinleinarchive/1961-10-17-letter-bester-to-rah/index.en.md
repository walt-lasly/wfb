# Letter from Alfred Bester to RAH (F&SF column invitation)

*Alfred Bester, writing from the offices of Fantasy & Science Fiction at 675 Madison Avenue, invites Heinlein to become a guest columnist for the magazine's book section. He and editor Bob Mills are offering $100 per column for opinionated, controversial essays of about 2,000 words on any aspect of SF books, writing, authors, themes, or style. Bester also extends the invitation to Ray Bradbury and Ted Sturgeon. The letter is signed "Alfie." The year is not stated but October 17 fell on a Tuesday in 1961, consistent with Bob Mills' tenure as F&SF editor (1958–1962).*

{{< figure src="bester_logo.png" >}}

{{< typewriter >}}

<p class="letter-date">Tuesday, October 17th</p>

Dear Bob:

In our efforts to make the book column of Fantasy & Science Fiction more stimulating, Bob Mills and I have persuaded the publisher to let us invite stellar guest columnists to participate and pay them $100 for a column.

Would you be interested in doing an essay or critique on any aspect of science fiction books/writing/authors/themes/style/ or what have you, about two thousand words long, and the more opinionated and controversial, the better? So far as I'm concerned there will be no holds barred, even if you want to slang me. I know that goes for Bob Mills, too, and I'm sure the publisher will only worry about the laws of the Federal mails.

So far I'm extending this invitation only to you, Ray Bradbury, and Ted Sturgeon. I do hope that you'll accept, and, perhaps, even be willing to do future columns to rebut the storms that your scintillating writing always raises.

All my best...

{{< figure src="signature_bester.png" alt="Alfie" caption="Alfie" >}}

Alfred Bester



675 Madison Avenue

New York 21, N. Y.

{{< /typewriter >}}

**Notes**

**Alfred Bester at Holiday** — By 1961 Bester had largely stepped back from writing SF and was working as a staff writer and editor at *Holiday*, the upscale travel monthly published by Curtis Publications at 477 Madison Avenue — which explains the letterhead on his follow-up reply.

**Bob Mills** — Robert P. Mills, editor of *The Magazine of Fantasy & Science Fiction* from 1958 to 1962 (succeeding Anthony Boucher). He previously served as Horace Gold's assistant at *Galaxy*.

**$100 per column** — A respectable fee for 1961, roughly equivalent to $1,000 in 2025 purchasing power.

**Ray Bradbury and Ted Sturgeon** — The other two writers Bester initially approached for the guest column. Bester's choice of targets shows he was aiming for the most distinctive literary voices in SF, not its most commercially safe ones.

**RAH declined** — His decision is confirmed by Bester's follow-up letter (written on a Sunday from Holiday's offices): "it's F&SF's loss."
