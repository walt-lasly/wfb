# Letter from Jack Kessie to RAH (Fredric Brown's Puppet Show)

*Playboy's Managing Editor Jack Kessie sends RAH a copy of the November 1962 issue at Fredric Brown's suggestion, so Heinlein can read Brown's story "Puppet Show." Kessie asks if Heinlein would care to offer any comments.*

<p>Mr. Robert A. Heinlein<br>

Dear Mr. Heinlein:

Enclosed is a copy of the November, 1962 issue of PLAYBOY. Fredric Brown felt that you might be interested in seeing his story, PUPPET SHOW.

We would certainly appreciate receiving any comments you might care to offer.

Cordially,

Jack Kessie  
Managing Editor

**Notes**

**Jack Kessie** — Managing Editor of *Playboy* in the early 1960s; he handled the magazine's literary correspondence on behalf of editor-in-chief Hugh Hefner.

**Fredric Brown / "Puppet Show"** — Brown (1906–1972) was a prolific mystery and SF writer known for his ingenious short-short stories. "Puppet Show" appeared in the November 1962 *Playboy* and won the Hugo Award for Best Short Story at Discon I (1963) — one of the few pieces published in *Playboy* to immediately win a Hugo.

**1776 Mesa, Broadmoor** — RAH's Colorado Springs address, a house in the Broadmoor neighborhood where he lived from the mid-1950s until the Heinleins relocated to Bonny Doon, California in the late 1960s.

**JJK/jdw** — Standard business-letter notation: the initials of the dictating author (Jack J. Kessie) followed by those of the typist.
