# Letter from RAH to Harlan Ellison (Seattle Worldcon, *Gentleman Junkie*)

*RAH writes to Harlan Ellison from home after the 1961 Seattle Worldcon, praising Ellison's platform presence, reacting at length to his book* Gentleman Junkie *and the story "Final Shtick," and weighing in on antisemitism, the death of science fiction, and Hollywood's demands on writers.*

{{< typewriter >}}

<p class="letter-date">6 September 1961</p>

Dear Harlan,

Damn it, I can't find your address—and I feel sure that I got it from you. However, we left in such haste that several papers were lost. We did not intend to leave yesterday morning, we planned to hang around for another day at Hyatt House, chewing the fat with any others who stayed over, then visit in the Puget Sound area—but Dr. Chris told Ginny to get me home at once and get me to bed. Which she did. And I let myself be handled like a sack of meal and didn't fight it and today I am relatively okay—thanks to Dr. Nourse shooting me full of penicillin I think, as yesterday morning as we left I had that horrid "drowning from the inside" feeling which means, to me, pneumonia. Missed it, thank God—but today Ginny is down with it. I hope nobody else caught it.

I may have to send this to ROGUE—they must have your address. I picked up the current ROGUE in Boise and read that nice review... but won't clip it unless you ask for it; I assume that you have it.

Brother, you are, without any competition, the toughest speaker I have ever had to follow. And believe me, I've followed some tough ones and tried to top them—and sometimes succeeded. You are the sharpest operator on a platform I've ever heard... and you are talking to a man who heard William Jennings Bryan before he got too old and who heard Woodrow Wilson when he was killing himself trying to sell the League of Nations. Different type of patter, of course—but you could sell snake oil to snakes.

How did I do? I wasn't there myself—I was running a high fever and once I stood up I was in a fog and didn't come out of it until I had been talking quite a while and realised that I had better chop it off and sit down. And damn near fell down instead.

GENTLEMAN JUNKIE: I haven't finished it yet, although I read large chunks of it on the flight back between doping off from fatigue and the bug. But it is clearly not a book to read to the children to put them to sleep at night; this stuff is raw corn liquor, bought out in the piney woods from a gentleman with a shot gun and wary eyes—not yet cooled from the still. I'm not even sure that it is "entertainment" but it certainly is high proof—you should serve a whiskbroom with each shot so that the customer can brush the saw dust off after he gets up from the floor.

I am trying to figure out where I fit into your definitive categorising of the human race. I don't feel hung-up, I'm not hungry, I don't think I'm a mollusk—so, by elimination, I must be one of the wet-rock people. Isn't there any fifth category?

FINAL SHTICK shook me up—since Frank stated flatly that it was autobiographical. Harlan, is this really how it was? In this country? Are we goyem so uniformly a bunch of bastards as you describe us to be? Look, I don't think it was that bad in my home town, Kansas City. Yes, I know that minorities had their troubles; this has been true in the human race at all times and in all places. But was it like that?

Harlan, until I was nearly grown I thought that anti-Semitism was something that happened in Russia, in the past. When I was a kid 1911–15 my three playmates were Saul and Solomon, twins, and Jack, a Catholic boy. Jack was considered somewhat odd because he went to a Catholic school, but Saul and Solomon were just like everybody else. My old man was an elder of our church; I never heard any anti-S. crap at home. Christ was a Jew, the Jews were the Chosen People—that was the way I got it at home... and never a hint that they were anything else than people who could, when they chose, See the Light. (Never any explanation as to why they were the Chosen People—and I still haven't figured out that angle—but no trace of the attitudes you describe so bitterly and vididly.) Of course, since then I have long since deserted the patent brand of theology I was brought up on—but it was not vicious stuff, even though I have left it.

Later, in junior high, my closest friend was Isidore Horoshem, born in Russia—and the nearest we came to any difference of opinion was once when I mentioned to him that the bishop had been at our house for dinner the night before... and he laughed at me and asked me if I *really* believed in all that crap about Jesus?

I was shocked, but it didn't bust up our friendship. Harlan, have you ever encountered anti-goyemism? It exists. I've encountered it, many times. Sure, as a member of the majority I can afford to brush it off—but I would sure as hell hate to be a goy in Tel Aviv!

Or a Christian in Cairo.

These things do cut both ways. And we goyem here in the USA can't all be such bastards or we would be having pogroms instead of football games on weekends. Want to join my neo-Zionist Society? The idea is to give the promised land back to the Caananites. If we can find any. If any were left alive after the rather thorough going over the Bible says they got.

Have you ever met a Parsee?

Hours later—the Parsees will have to wait; I must close this and take the miserable tag end of this cold to bed. You can see that FINAL SHTICK really affected me or I would not have gone on about it. But, please, let me say this one thing in defense of us gentiles: the great majority of us are not given to discrimination against Jews, in our actions and in our hearts, and a lot of us (of which I am one) will not be silent and tolerate it under any circumstances at all; instead we actively fight it at every opportunity because we hate it... but this is not entirely our problem and we goyem cannot solve it completely as long as there is a Jewish mother anywhere who opposes her son marrying a shicksa. Discrimination cannot be completely abolished on one side unless it is completely abolished on the other side as well. Both. This is not just a statement of fact concerning one group and one problem, but a general law of human psychology—it applies with equal force to Hindus & Muslims, Tamil & Sinhalese, you name it.

So, until that last Jewish mother accepts with open arms and open heart a goy son-in-law, there is a limit to what gentiles of good will can accomplish. But please don't ever forget that we are not all sons of bitches, nor even most of us, else we would be making lamp shades.

<p class="section-break">✲ ✲ ✲</p>

GENTLEMAN JUNKIE: last 4 lines p.11 & 1st 16 of p.12, in your preface—I agree with every word of it and it scares me, too. It makes me sick at my stomach and scares me silly—not personally as I have already lived longer than a man had a right to expect to live as short a time ago as the decade just before I was born. But for my beloved country. Harlan, I see us sunk into decadence that I fear will destroy us—a decadence of no moral values, of what's-in-it-for-me?, of what's-the-angle?, of blank surprise that anybody should even expect honesty, of prefering security to challenge, of shoddy work and shrugged shoulders and admiration for the smart guy who gets away with it, of caring more for the opinions of the gang, the crowd, the neighbors, than of what one's own conscience says.

But I needn't repeat it; you've already said it and I agree with you to nineteen decimal places.

Same subject, different key: You wanna know "what killed science fiction?" I'll tell you what killed science fiction: writers writing cheap stories for cheap people for a fast buck, that's what killed it. Stories they didn't really mean but had an "angle," tailored to what the writer thought the editor was buying or what they thought the public wanted to hear now. Cheapjack stuff, for a shoddy trade. Gimmicks.

Only science fiction isn't killed and can't be killed—along comes some new writer telling his stories his way, blazing with his own inner honesty... it'll sell, it'll sell; good merchandise will always sell, there is always a market for quality. His stuff will sell—and some of the critics will roast him and some of the pipsqueak fans and aspirant pseudo-professionals will tear him apart and point out that he doesn't do it the "right" way and who does he think he is, Jesus Christ? (To which the only appropriate answer has always been: "One of our boys made it.") And presently the noise will die down and they will start imitating him—oh, most accurately... all but the heart and soul that was in it—and then presently somebody will ask again, "What killed science fiction?"

But good science fiction is never dead, good fiction of any sort is never dead—it is just scarce as hell at all times. A writer must do it himself, out of his own inner fire (as you have written these stories), his own stuff his own way, with the steadfastness to ignore editors and critics alike, even ignoring the reading public considered as a vast mass average and talking only to that reader (whether he be one or ten million) who will listen to what you got to say, without sugar coating, without a kowtowing to popular opinion and prejudices.

This why I don't write for movies, television, or radio, because this is no job for a trained seal (to write honestly, I mean) and in those media there is always someone and sometimes many who can and do overrule the writer and change what he has to say. Oh, I'll sell rights to any of these media and do—but I won't write script for them unless I control the final form... which happens less frequently than quintuplets.

(I made one mowos movie once—and the only reason I was not ashamed to have my name on it was because the director was a man of integrity. But I learned what I was up against and have not been tempted since. Not too long ago I turned down $30,000 for six weeks work in Hollywood... and it took me less than five seconds to say "No" and another ten minutes to explain why in terms that would hurt no one's feelings, as it was an honest offer politely made. But I ain't that hungry. I won't ever be that hungry because there are other sorts of honest work open to me if I ever dry up as a writer. I won't deal with an editor who revises my MSS... and I sure as hell won't work under conditions where a writer is supposed to be a tame dog.)

(Reminds me— I was in Laguna Beach, visiting Hank and Catherine Kuttner at the time at which I lost my motion-picture virginity and was telling them about the tame writer the banker on the picture kept in his office to put on paper the banker's great ideas for changing the script. Hank blinked at me gnomishly and said, "I didn't know writers could live in captivity" and Catherine said, "Oh, they'll sometimes live—but they won't breed"... and Ginny guffawed so loudly that she spilt her drink.)

I meant to shut this off and must. I am delighted to have gotten acquainted with you at last (our meeting in Pittsburgh was too kaleidoscopic and frenzied to count) and I look forward to knowing you and your beautiful and charming wife better—I didn't see enough of her in Seattle, but I never do have as much of the company of a pretty girl as I would like; this is a law of nature. Our address is on this letter, our telephone number is MElrose 2-4892. It is not always possible for us to take house guests but there are adequate motels nearby ("restricted clientele" of course—they sell marijuana to minors only if they have cash—the truth is that I don't think we have any of that discrimination poison in our town; it is a tourist trap interested only in your wallet, not your ancestry). We hope that you both will someday be coming through this backwater and will favor us with your company.

I had a real ball in Seattle despite being ill—and you had a lot to do with its being such.

All the best,

**Notes**

**Hyatt House** — The hotel in Seattle where many 19th World Science Fiction Convention (Seacon, August 31–September 4, 1961) attendees stayed after the convention ended.

**Dr. Nourse** — Dr. Alan E. Nourse (1928–1992), SF writer, physician, and longtime Heinlein family friend. He appears several times in RAH's correspondence as the Heinleins' go-to doctor when traveling in the Pacific Northwest.

**ROGUE** — *Rogue* magazine (1956–1966), a men's magazine competitor to *Playboy* that published serious fiction and literary journalism; Harlan Ellison was a frequent contributor in this period.

***Gentleman Junkie*** — Ellison's 1961 short-story collection published by Regency Books. The preface referenced here is Ellison's own introduction to the volume, which apparently included a sharp critique of American moral decay.

**"Final Shtick"** — A story by Ellison included in *Gentleman Junkie*, reportedly autobiographical, dealing with anti-Semitism. RAH's extended response to it takes up much of this letter.

**Frank** — Likely Ellison's editor or a mutual acquaintance who told RAH the story was autobiographical; not identified with certainty from internal evidence.

**Goyem / goy / shicksa** — Yiddish terms: *goyem* (non-Jews, plural of *goy*); *shicksa* (a non-Jewish woman). RAH uses them throughout as a mark of comfort and familiarity with Ellison's cultural vocabulary.

**Hank and Catherine Kuttner** — Henry Kuttner (1915–1958) and C. L. Moore (1911–1987), a husband-and-wife SF writing team and friends of the Heinleins. Kuttner died in 1958, so RAH is recalling a past visit to Laguna Beach, California, where the Kuttners lived.

**"mowos movie"** — Typographical quirk in the original; almost certainly "movies" mistyped. RAH likely refers to the 1950 film *Destination Moon*, for which he co-wrote the screenplay.

**$30,000 for six weeks in Hollywood** — RAH had fielded and declined multiple Hollywood offers by the early 1960s; this specific offer is not identified in published sources.

**MElrose 2-4892** — A telephone number in the MELrose exchange (ME = 63), associated with the Los Angeles area in the early 1960s, suggesting the Heinleins were residing in or near LA at this date — consistent with their Colorado Springs / Santa Cruz transition period.

**Pittsburgh meeting** — Ellison and RAH had apparently met previously at a convention in Pittsburgh (likely the 1960 Pittcon Worldcon).
